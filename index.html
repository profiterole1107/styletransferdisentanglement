IMPROVING ZERO-SHOT STYLE TRANSFER TEXT-TO-SPEECH BY DISENTANGLED FINE-GRAINED STYLE MODELING

Submission to JASA Express Letters, 2025

Abstract

Recent zero-shot style-transfer speech synthesis methods have shown promising results, and have been able to address adaptation to unseen speaking styles. While most state-of-the-art approaches achieve generalization to new speakers and styles with either very large models or large corpora, achieving the same generalization with a smaller model remains an open challenge. To this end, we first propose a novel zero-shot method that uses the small Generspeech backbone with the addition of a new fine-grained style encoder. Second, to perform disentanglement among speakers, global/fine-grained styles, and content embeddings, we introduce a specific mutual information minimization loss. Third, to further disentangle style from speaker and boost style embedding diversity, we introduce a maximum-mean-discrepancy-guided cycle-consistency loss. Experimental results show that we outperform the baseline zero-shot style-transfer methods with an average style preference of 58% and a prosody similarity PMOS score of 3.64 on the VCTK test set.
